# Project Title - Comprehensive Financial Data Analysis
Analyzing the profitability and leverage of companies from various industries by computing and visualizing financial ratios.

# Financial Terminologies - 
1) D/E Ratio - The Debt-to-Equity (D/E) ratio is a fundamental financial metric used to evaluate a company’s financial leverage and its ability to cover outstanding debts. Essentially, it shows the proportion of a company's assets that are financed by creditors versus what is owned by shareholders.
    High Ratio - Generally indicates that a company has been aggressive in financing its growth with debt. This can result in higher earnings if the company grows, but it also     increases the risk of bankruptcy if earnings don't cover interest costs
    Low Ratio - Suggests a more conservative approach, relying more on investor money. While safer, it might mean the company isn't taking full advantage of the growth             opportunities that leverage can provide.
2) Shareholder Equity - The amount of money that would be returned to shareholders if all assets were liquidated and all debts paid off.
3) Gross-Margin Ratio - The Gross Margin Ratio (also known as the Gross Profit Margin) is a profitability metric that shows the percentage of revenue that exceeds the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS). Formula: (Total Revenue - COGS)/Total Revenue 
4) Leverage Ratios - Leverage ratios are financial metrics used to determine to what extent a company is using borrowed money (debt) to fund its operations and growth. Example D/E ratio, equity multiplier, others
